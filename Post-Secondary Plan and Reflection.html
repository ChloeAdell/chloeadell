Reflection

During my time at SECHS, several experiences have contributed to my growth, development, and success. One of the most important factors has been the dual enrollment opportunity with Lone Star College. Taking college-level courses while still in high school taught me how to manage my time effectively and pushed me to become more independent and self-motivated. The academic challenges I faced in classes like General Chemistry II, encouraged me to develop strong study habits and to seek help when needed, whether from teachers, tutors, or peers. The support I received from SECHS staff, who genuinely care about student success, also played a big role in keeping me on track. Another major influence was my involvement in extracurricular activities, where I learned teamwork, leadership, and communication skills. Working on group projects taught me how to collaborate with others and value different perspectives. I've also learned to advocate for myself and take responsibility for my learning.
